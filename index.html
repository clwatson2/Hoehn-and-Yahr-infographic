Hoehn and Yahr Scale

Parkinson's Disease Severity Classification
1
Stage
Unilateral Involvement
Symptoms on one side of the body only. Minimal or no functional impairment. Symptoms are mild and may include tremor, rigidity, or bradykinesia affecting one limb or one side of the body.
2
Stage
Bilateral Involvement Without Balance Impairment
Symptoms on both sides of the body. Posture and balance not affected. Patient is still fully independent in activities of daily living but may experience increased difficulty with tasks.
3
Stage
Mild to Moderate Disease With Balance Impairment
First signs of impaired balance and coordination. Falls may occur. Slowness of movement is evident. Mild to moderate restriction of activities, but patient remains physically independent.
4
Stage
Severe Disability
Symptoms are severe and limiting. Patient requires assistance with activities of daily living. Able to walk and stand unassisted, but may need walking aid. Cannot live alone without assistance.
5
Stage
Complete Dependence
Most advanced stage. Patient is wheelchair-bound or bedridden unless aided. Constant nursing care required for all activities. Unable to stand or walk even with assistance.
Hoehn, M. M., & Yahr, M. D. (1967). Parkinsonism: onset, progression and mortality. Neurology, 17(5), 427-442.
Note: Modified Hoehn and Yahr scale includes stages 1.5 and 2.5. This infographic presents the original five-stage classification.
